The Problem: Fake News in Our Community

Fake news has become a serious issue in today's digital world. Many people unknowingly share false information, which spreads quickly across social media platforms. In our community, misinformation can cause confusion, fear, and misunderstanding.

One of the main reasons fake news spreads is the lack of verification. Users often rely on headlines or images without checking if the source is credible. This leads to the rapid circulation of misleading or completely false content.

62% of Filipinos get news from social media
87% don't verify sources before sharing

Our Solution: TruthCheck AI

To address this issue, we developed TruthCheck AI, a prototype system that helps users verify information before sharing it. The system allows users to submit text or images and receive an analysis of whether the content is likely true, suspicious, or fake.

📥

Text & Image Input

AI-Assisted Analysis

Tips to Avoid Fake News

🔍

Check the Source

Always verify who published the information. Is it from a credible news organization?

📅

Look at the Date

Old news can be misleading if shared out of context. Check when it was published.

🔗

Cross-Check Multiple Sources

Don't rely on just one source. Compare with other trusted news outlets.

🧠

Think Before You Share

Take a moment to verify before clicking share. You're responsible for what you spread.
